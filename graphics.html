The Sage Labyrinth | Spring 2020

This project is a collection of pictures I've taken at various landmarks within RPI's Russell Sage Lab building.

By reading across the columns and rows, one can virtually traverse this academic space. In many ways, this building is a maze. Constructed in parts and given an annex, there are stairs that seem never-ending or hallways that lead in circles. This piece is meant to highlight key experiences of being a Humanities & Social Science major, such as the sculpture gallery and "hallway of creativity".

Photography | Spring 2020

The following compositions are a series of exercises that demostrate various visual design principles, such as the relationship between a figure and the fore/background or closure in a piece.

Figure-Ground: Taken in the back room of RPI's design studio, this composition is meant to showcase the depth of the space using light and shadow to differentiate elements in the foreground vs. the background.

Closure: This composition was created by rolling and bending an alumninum strip at my university's fabrication lab.

Scale: The colors and saturation of this composition were altered to further "push" the illusion.

Pattern: This photo was taken at one of RPI's loading docks, as it highlights a pattern through repetition and is contrasted by a change in color.
