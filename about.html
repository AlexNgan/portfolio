Biography

>> [PDF] Resume

I graduated from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute with a BS in Communication and Design, Innovation, and Society, a unique combination of engineering and disciplines in the liberal arts, including social science and design.

The DIS program focuses on social design—that is, not just how we can create something, but also why we choose to design a product a certain way. As an engineer, I’m fascinated in product design and dynamic solutions for complex issues. As such, I’m interested in working with innovative companies that allow me to apply my skills in a field where I can better the quality of people’s lives.

During my time at RPI, I’ve previously done several design projects in which I analyzed existing systems for issues and gauged the efficacy of potential solutions. One of these examined the accessibility of kitchen knives and compared their designs. Ultimately, I re-designed the grip to both encourage proper technique and make the process easier for someone with physical limitations. Through ethnographic research, observation, and research into pre-existing designs, I created and modeled the new grip. This was then reproduced in several materials and iterated. In the process, I engaged with everyday users, kitchen supply stores, and professional chefs to bring my ideas and their experiences into the development of a new product.

Additionally, I’ve had experience in technical design on a robotics team for four years. I've analyzed problems, prototyped solutions with 3D printers and laser cutters, created CAD assemblies and engineering drawings, manufactured parts (both by hand and on a CNC mill) - basically brainstorming and designing a robot from scratch, then building it. As such, I’ve developed skills on both the technical and client nav aspects of design and problem-solving.

I'm a New York native, born in Manhattan, and grew up in Queens and the Bronx. As such, I've got a passion for improving the public transportation and accessibility, having spent a great deal of my life riding around in buses and subways.

My hobbies include drawing, programming, writing, and graphic design. The former two were developed a great deal during my time as an editor for my high school's newspaper, The Science Survey. I've also got a lot of background in CAD and mechanical design with FRC robotics, both as a student and as a mentor.
