Biography

Hi, I'm a third year undergraduate student at the Rensselear Polytechnic Institute. Currently, I'm working towards a dual degree in Communication and Design, Innovation, and Society, a unique combination of engineering and disciplines in the liberal arts, including social science and design. The DIS program focuses on social design—that is, not just how we can create something, but also why we choose to design a product a certain way. As an engineer, I’m fascinated in product design and dynamic solutions for complex issues. As such, I’m interested in working with companies that delve into innovation. After completing my undergraduate degree, I hope to apply my skills in a field where I can better the quality of people’s lives.

I'm a New York native, born in Manhattan, and grew up in Queens and the Bronx. As such, I've got a passion for improving the public transportation and accessibility, having spent a great deal of my life riding around in buses and subways.

My hobbies include drawing, programming, writing, and graphic design. The former two were developed a great deal during my time as an editor for my high school's newspaper, The Science Survey. I've also got a lot of background in CAD and mechanical design with FRC robotics, both as a student and as a mentor.

What is TITAN DESIGNS?

TITAN is my personal design brand; this website is both a personal website as well as an online portfolio. All in all, this is a snapshot of my foray into design. With that being said, what is design?

Personally, while I feel that design is a tool to address issues, a great deal of what it can do is rooted in the philosophy behind a product. Without a deeper understanding of the problem, most "solutions" are just bandaid responses to a symptom. At the end of the day, pretty much everyone can design and problem-solve, but a good designer is someone who can create a conversation with their creations. After all, the first step in solving the root of a problem is making people aware of its source.
