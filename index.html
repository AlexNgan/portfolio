Current Work // Alex works for ZOLL Medical in their Acute Care Technology division on medical devices like ventilators, defibrillators, patient monitors, and AEDs that are used all over the world. He's been with ZOLL since 2021 and the part of the job he enjoys most is actually getting to embed himself in the lives of end users, going on ride-alongs with EMS or shadowing nurses who use these medical devices.

Education // Alex graduated from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in 2020 with a dual BS in Communication and Design, Innovation, and Society, a unique combination of engineering and disciplines in the liberal arts, including social science and design. His senior capstone focused on expanding options for transgender and gender non-comforming individuals within the sex toy market. While this space has become less destigmatized, there is still a great amount of taboo surrounding both transgender bodies and sexual pleasure.

Philosophy // Design is a powerful tool to address issues, but a great deal of what we can do should be embedded into the philosophy behind a product. Without a deeper understanding of the problem, most "solutions" are just bandaid responses to a symptom. At the end of the day, pretty much everyone can design and problem-solve, but a good designer is someone who can create a conversation with their work. As a designer, Alex is focused on accessibility and inclusive design. His goal is to apply his skills in a field where he can better the quality of people’s lives. His background includes a mix of industrial design, graphic design, and mechanical engineering. Much of Alex's engineering experience is rooted in the time he's spent doing competitive robotics, where he taught himself to program, work with electronics and pneumatic systems, design components, and operate equipment in a machine shop.
