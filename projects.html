Historical Design Series | Fall 2018

Previous Next

Operating off a design brief, I was constrained with the task of emulating the style of prominent ceramics artist Eva Zeisel. This model was entirely created from a series of sketches and ultimately modeled in a CAD program. Comprised of a ceramic body and a glass base, this coffee pot invokes Zeisel's elegant design while incorporating pour-over components for brewing freshly ground coffee beans.

Integrating Renewable Energy into the U.S. Power Grid | Spring 2018

This report was an analysis of the infrastructure of existing transmission mechanisms and ways in which new energy sources may be integrated. Renewable energy sources were compared with older nonrenewables in a cost-benefits analysis. We also examined costs of adapting the grid from an economical, environmental, and social standpoint.

An Analysis of Accessibility in NYC's Public Transit | Fall 2017

An analysis of the design and efficacy of the New York City transit system in serving the needs of those with physical disabilities. Using data and reports from the MTA, censuses, and previous studies, I compared the city's transit system with others of the same time period, as well as developments that have been made since then. This report also includes and analysis of current accomodations (such as ramps and elevators) and reccomendations for integrating these.
